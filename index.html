The Founder

Meet Amy Rohan

Amy Rohan founded Seven Feathers by taking stock of what she already had: land with a rare, quiet power; decades of experience guiding and caring for people; and an innate sense of how to hold space for healing. She built from those gifts intentionally, creating a place where both land and practice meet to help people slow down, heal, and reconnect.

With more than 25 years of experience across legal operations, nonprofit programs, and community care, Amy has managed complex budgets, led cross-sector teams, and collaborated with legal, financial, and community partners in high-accountability environments. Yet the work that shaped her most happened one-on-one, supporting individuals and families through life's most challenging seasons, skills she brings to every guest experience.

A certified Reiki Master, Amy has offered energy sessions to guests and will integrate that practice fully into the Seven Feathers retreat experience, creating a container for restoration grounded in both intention and expertise.

She creates spaces where respect, sovereignty, and belonging are felt in every detail, where the land and the people it holds are honored, and where those who come are given the chance to simply arrive, fully seen, and quietly transformed.

Market Analysis
The Wellness Tourism Opportunity
Global Market

The global wellness tourism market was valued at approximately $950 billion in 2024, with projections ranging from $1.7 to $2.0 trillion by 2030 to 2034. Growth rates range from 6 to 11% annually across major research sources. North America accounts for roughly 35 to 42% of the global market.

Key Market Trends
  • Pet-Inclusive Travel: 78% of American pet owners travel with their pets annually. 52% of all travelers factor pet accommodations into their plans. Seven Feathers' no-size-restriction, no-fee pet policy is a direct competitive advantage.
  • Homesteading & Self-Sufficiency: Post-pandemic interest in gardening, sustainable living, and food self-sufficiency continues to drive demand for hands-on educational experiences.
  • Remote Work & Extended Stays: High-speed internet combined with a secluded natural setting creates conditions for extended stays and remote work retreats, a rapidly growing segment.
  • Nature-Based Restoration: Demand for forest bathing, earth-based ceremony, and outdoor wellness programming has grown significantly since 2020 and continues to expand.
Location Advantage: Ashe County, NC

Ashe County is a rural mountain community in the northwestern corner of North Carolina, bordering Virginia and Tennessee. It offers what guests are actively seeking: genuine seclusion, quiet, and wide-open land, with lower operating costs that allow competitive pricing and healthy margins.

The lower cost of living and land values in Ashe County also allow the retreat to offer competitive pricing while maintaining healthy margins, a structural advantage over competitors in higher-cost markets.

Competitive Position
  • Small, intimate, owner-operated sanctuary, as distinct from large commercial retreat centers
  • Venue rental model serving independent facilitators who need dedicated space, scalable beyond any one person's calendar
  • Pet-friendly property with genuine space, fenced acreage, creek access, no restrictions
  • Accessible pricing without sacrificing authenticity or experience quality
  • On-site Reiki Master available as an optional add-on for venue rental groups
  • Multi-revenue model combining nightly vacation rentals, retreat venue rental, and experiential programming
Risk Mitigation
Built-In Safety Factors

Every element of the expansion plan has been designed to limit downside exposure and build on demonstrated, real demand.

Existing Revenue Base

The property is already generating rental income with a 4.96-star Airbnb rating. This is an expansion of an operating business with proven demand. Revenue continues at every stage of the buildout.

Proven Demand

The property has generated consistent rental income across multiple years, reaching $14,487 in a single year with only part-time availability and no on-site hosting. Under active, full-time management beginning in 2025, the property reached $10,290 in a partial year with significant growth capacity ahead.

Phased Approach

Investment scales with demonstrated success at each stage. Phase 1 requires minimal capital ($2,000 to $5,000) and begins generating incremental revenue immediately. Each subsequent phase is funded in part by the revenue generated in the prior phase.

Owner-Operated Advantage

The owner lives on the property full-time and serves as host, venue manager, and retreat coordinator. This eliminates property management fees (typically 15 to 25% of gross revenue) and ensures the authentic, personal hospitality that drives the 4.96-star rating. Owner-operator compensation is budgeted into the capital raise, ensuring full-time, undivided attention to the business during the critical ramp-up period. This costs significantly less than third-party management and produces demonstrably better results.

Diversified Income Streams

The business draws from multiple independent revenue sources: nightly rentals, Reiki sessions, day experiences, retreat programming, and community events. Each stream contributes on its own terms, creating stability and room to grow across all of them.

Eco-Friendly Infrastructure

Zome dwellings use bio-ceramic MgO panels rated fireproof (to 2,200°F), waterproof, mold-proof, and pest-proof with a 140 mph wind rating. They assemble in 5 to 10 days with no heavy machinery and are placed on non-permanent foundations, simplifying permitting and reducing construction risk.

Transparency Commitment

All market statistics cited in this document are sourced from published industry research. Financial projections will be updated quarterly as real data becomes available. Investors will receive regular, honest communication about performance, including what is working and what needs adjustment.

Investment Structure
Ways to Partner

Partnership structures are flexible and designed around what each person brings and what they need in return. A formal operating agreement defines rights and responsibilities for all parties.

  • Equity Stake: Hold an ownership percentage in the retreat business, sharing in both the upside as revenues grow and the long-term value of what we are building together. Percentage negotiated based on investment amount.
  • Revenue Share: Receive an agreed percentage of gross or net revenue over a defined period. Can be tied to specific revenue streams. Transparent quarterly reporting as standard.
  • Loan with Interest: A straightforward loan structure with agreed interest and a repayment timeline. Repayment begins when revenue milestones are reached, with an option to convert to equity.
  • Non-Financial Partnership: Skills, networks, labor, materials, or expertise that contribute meaningfully. Marketing, construction, wellness facilitation, or network connections all have real value here.
The best agreements are built around what each person brings and what they need in return. If you have an idea that does not fit neatly into these categories, bring it.
Exit Strategy
Path to Return

Investors have multiple clear options for realizing a return on their investment:

  • Sale as Turnkey Business: The property and retreat operation, once established with multiple revenue streams and a strong reputation, can be sold as a turnkey retreat business. Comparable properties in the Blue Ridge command a significant premium.
  • Refinance to Return Capital: Once the property has appreciated and the business demonstrates consistent revenue, refinancing can return investor principal while the retreat continues operating.
  • Ongoing Profit Sharing: Investors who prefer long-term participation can receive ongoing distributions as the business grows and matures across Years 2 through 5 and beyond.
  • Investor Buyout: After a successful proof of concept, the owner may seek to refinance or raise additional capital to buy out early investors at an agreed valuation.

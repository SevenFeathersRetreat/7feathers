The Boone Market Advantage

AirDNA, the leading short-term rental analytics platform, classifies Lansing, NC within the Boone market and assigns it a Market Score of 89 out of 100. That is a meaningful designation. Boone is one of the most recognized mountain destinations in the Southeast, drawing consistent year-round demand from guests across the region. Lansing benefits from that brand recognition and search traffic while offering something Boone itself cannot: genuine seclusion, open acreage, and the unhurried quality that travelers come to the mountains to find in the first place.

Why This Market Position Matters

The geodome and unique stay listings operating in this region are already proving the model. Glamping Wild in Creston, a direct geographic neighbor, operates three geodomes at $199 to $217 per night with full five-star ratings, a private bathhouse, wetlands preserve, and walking trails. Their success is not accidental, and it is not far away. It is the clearest demonstration that the audience for exactly this kind of experience is already here, already booking, and willing to pay for it.

Seven Feathers has two structural advantages those properties do not: acreage that supports a full retreat model rather than individual accommodation units, and an on-site owner-operator whose background in wellness, hospitality, and community care creates a guest experience that goes beyond the physical stay.

The current ADR of $120 is what this property earns while under-furnished, limited to one platform, and not yet positioned as the retreat it is becoming. The comparable market says the ceiling is well above $200 per unit per night. The gap between those two numbers is the investment opportunity.
